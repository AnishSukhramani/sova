## Lead Scoring Architecture

A rigorous scoring model for Sova should not be a simple average of fit and intent with crude multipliers. It should reflect what the literature and market evidence both suggest: **workflow pain plus timing beats static firmographics**, and the buying centre means a lead is hotter when Sova can speak to both the cheque-writer and the daily user. citeturn21search5turn26academia4turn26academia5turn26academia1

I recommend the following weight model:

| Signal family | Weight | Why |
|---|---:|---|
| Practice fit | 20 | You still need the right size, specialty mix, independence and workflow relevance |
| Operational pain | 25 | Hiring, reviews, answer failures and schedule leakage most closely map to the product’s value proposition |
| Timing / lifecycle | 20 | Openings, ownership changes, expansion and new associates create genuine buying windows |
| First-party intent | 15 | Site visits, demo-line calls and branded search spikes show active evaluation, not inferred curiosity |
| Technographic opportunity | 10 | Outdated or incomplete comms stack increases odds of fit and sharpens the displacement angle |
| Human-route access | 5 | Champion presence and identifiable office-manager/owner contacts improve conversion odds |
| Market pressure / geography | 5 | DSO density, metro call volume and labour tightness matter, but should not overpower direct signals |

```text
0.25*Pain +
0.20*Timing +
0.15*FirstPartyIntent +
0.10*TechnographicOpportunity +
0.05*HumanRoute +
0.05*Geography
```

Then apply **bounded modifiers**, not open-ended multipliers:

- **+8** champion moved from an existing client  
- **+6** ownership transfer / sold practice  
- **+5** new practice opening or second location  
- **+4** repeated live-answer failure or after-hours failure  
- **+3** DSO opened nearby  
- **+3** first-party demo-line interaction  
- **−4** clear enterprise/DSO central procurement if Sova is not pursuing enterprise motion  
- **−4** strong incumbent footprint with positive evidence and no current pain  
- **−6** likely closed/inactive practice or dead phone / stale web presence  
- **−3** obvious mismatch in specialty, scale or call economics

For time decay, use a half-life function rather than a hard cut-off:

```text
Decayed Signal Value = Raw Value * e^(-ln(2) * days_since_signal / half_life)
```

Recommended half-lives:

| Signal | Half-life |
|---|---:|
| Demo-line call / identified website visit | 7 days |
| Job posting / active front-desk hiring | 14 days |
| Review complaint spike / live-answer test failure | 21 days |
| Branded-search spike / webinar attendance | 21 days |
| Ownership transfer / broker listing disappearance | 60 days |
| New practice opening / NPI delta / SBA loan | 90 days |
| Technographic gap | 180 days |

A lead should only be marked **HOT** when all of the following are true:

| Requirement | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Composite score | **78+** |
| Fit score | **65+** |
| Pain or timing | At least **one major pain signal** *and* **one timing/intent signal** |
| Recency | One key signal in the last **30 days** |
| Reachability | Owner or office-manager route identified |
| No major disqualifier | Not obviously closed, fully centralised, or clearly out of segment |

Geography should affect scoring, but modestly. I would add **+2 to +5** for metro and affluent suburban catchments where call volume, cosmetic/elective revenue, and competition make every missed call more expensive. I would add **+3 to +6** for counties with heavier DSO penetration, because the independent-owner urgency story strengthens when corporate competition is nearby. I would subtract **−2 to −4** for very low-volume rural accounts unless other signals are extremely strong or the practice is part of a growth cluster.
